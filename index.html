Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

30.01% (+5.01pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 30.01%, Kazakhstan has low awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating significant opportunity for growth.
  • The +5.01pp year-over-year growth suggests strong positive momentum in awareness levels.
  • This substantial growth rate (+5.01pp) is among the highest in emerging markets, indicating effective penetration of marketing messages.
  • Marketing should maintain strong awareness-building efforts as the primary focus for this market.
  • The positive trend in awareness provides a solid foundation for building the marketing funnel, though significant investment is still required to reach moderate awareness levels.

Consideration

13.72% (+1.72pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 13.72%, consideration is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • The +1.72pp increase shows a positive trend that should be built upon.
  • This suggests a significant gap between awareness (30.01%) and consideration (13.72%), indicating that while Kazakhstani travelers are becoming more aware of Abu Dhabi, many still aren't actively considering it as a destination.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing the specific barriers that prevent aware consumers from considering Abu Dhabi, particularly expense concerns (41%), previous visitation (24%), and visa process difficulty (22%).
  • The low consideration rate indicates a need for targeted messaging that addresses the specific concerns and preferences of the Kazakhstani market.

Intent

8.66% (+2.66pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 8.66%, intent is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The +2.66pp increase shows a strong positive trend that is encouraging for future growth.
  • This suggests that even among those who consider Abu Dhabi, relatively few have immediate plans to visit.
  • The significant increase in intent (+2.66pp) is a positive sign that tactical marketing efforts are beginning to drive conversion.
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking (visa process, flight availability, etc.).
  • The low intent level with positive growth suggests that tactical, conversion-focused campaigns are beginning to work and should be expanded.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.63 (+0.13)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 8.66% ÷ 13.72% = 0.63

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.63, the conversion from consideration to intent is good, indicating that 63% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The increase of +0.13 suggests a significant improvement in conversion efficiency.
  • This indicates that while both consideration and intent are low in absolute terms, the conversion between these stages is relatively efficient.
  • The good ratio suggests that once Kazakhstani travelers consider Abu Dhabi, they have a reasonable likelihood of converting to actual intent to visit.
  • Marketing should leverage this efficient conversion by focusing on moving more consumers into the consideration phase, as they are likely to convert well to intent.
  • The significant improvement in this ratio (+0.13) is a very positive sign, indicating that marketing efforts are successfully addressing barriers between consideration and intent.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Kazakhstan market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

3.3 days

The average number of nights Kazakhstani travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 3.3-day average stay indicates a short-to-medium length vacation timeframe, typical of many Central Asian markets.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 3-4 day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend-plus packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 3-4 day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The relatively short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time.

Booking Window

49 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Kazakhstani travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 49-day booking window is moderate, indicating a market that plans trips with reasonable lead time.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 1.5-2 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for considered decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Early booking incentives are likely to be effective for this market.
  • The moderate booking window suggests that both digital and traditional media can be effective, as there is sufficient time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This planning period also provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements over time.
Strategic Example:

For the March peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in January to align with the 49-day booking window. Early booking discounts offered 60+ days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging even earlier commitments.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Mar & Oct

The months with the highest volume of Kazakhstani hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Kazakhstani guests.

Business Implications:
  • March (8,229 guests) and October (7,671 guests) represent the clear peak season for Kazakhstani travelers.
  • This bi-modal peak aligns with favorable weather conditions and Kazakhstani holiday periods.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (February, April, September, November) to extend the peak periods.
  • Summer months represent a significant growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting indoor activities and special summer offers.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.4 (moderate seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Kazakhstan travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if October has a travel query index of 5.31 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 4.5, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.4 (5.31 ÷ 4.5).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.4 value indicates that the market has moderate seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 40% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant seasonal marketing strategies, but not so extreme as to require radical shifts in approach throughout the year.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 49-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The moderate seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year, but with less extreme variations than markets with higher seasonality indices.

Media Elasticity

0.09 (low impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Kazakhstan market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.09 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 0.9%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with low efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive but weak (well below 0.3).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI but with limited efficiency.
  • Even large increases in media spend will produce relatively modest growth in travel queries.
  • The low elasticity suggests that the Kazakhstan market is not highly responsive to media campaigns alone, indicating that other factors significantly influence travel decisions.
  • This low responsiveness suggests that media investments should be carefully targeted and complemented with other marketing strategies for this market.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 50% for a campaign, you can expect approximately a 4.5% increase in travel queries (50% × 0.09 = 4.5%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations, and suggests that media impressions alone may not be the most efficient driver of travel queries for this market.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.78 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Kazakhstan travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.78 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with good confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with greater certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.78) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±8.2%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±8.2%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±8.2% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 7.5 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 6.9 and 8.1 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Kazakhstan market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Kazakhstani travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Expense: 41% (+8pp) believe it is too expensive
  • Previous Visitation: 24% (+12pp) have already visited Abu Dhabi before
  • Visa Process: 22% (-5pp) find the visa process difficult
  • Flight Availability: 18% (-3pp) limited direct flights or inconvenient connections
  • Climate: 15% (+2pp) concerns about heat, especially during summer months
Business Implications:
  • Expense (41%, +8pp): This significant increase indicates growing price sensitivity in the Kazakhstan market. Marketing should emphasize value offerings and affordable options to address this perception.
  • Previous Visitation (24%, +12pp): The substantial increase suggests that many Kazakhstani travelers who have visited Abu Dhabi are not motivated to return. This indicates a need to develop and promote new attractions, experiences, and reasons to revisit.
  • Visa Process (22%, -5pp): The decrease suggests improvements in visa processing, but it remains a significant barrier. Continued efforts to streamline the visa process and communicate these improvements are needed.
  • Flight Availability (18%, -3pp): The slight decrease indicates some improvement in flight connections, but this remains a barrier for nearly one-fifth of potential visitors. Continued work with airlines to improve connectivity is important.
  • Climate (15%, +2pp): The slight increase in climate concerns suggests a need for better communication about indoor activities and seasonal variations in Abu Dhabi's weather.
Strategic Recommendation:

Based on these barriers, a "Value Discovery" campaign highlighting affordable experiences and packages (addressing the "Expense" barrier) combined with promotion of new attractions and experiences (addressing the "Previous Visitation" barrier) would address the two most significant barriers to visitation from the Kazakhstan market. This could be complemented with clear information about the simplified visa process to address the third most significant barrier.

Increasing Barriers

3 worsening factors

Barriers to visitation that have shown significant deterioration over time, representing challenges that need to be addressed.

Worsening Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Previous Visitation: 24% (+12pp) have already visited Abu Dhabi before
  • Expense: 41% (+8pp) believe it is too expensive
  • Perception as Destination for Wealthy: 14% (+6pp) believe it is only for wealthy people
Business Implications:
  • The substantial increase in "Previous Visitation" (+12pp) as a barrier suggests that the destination is not providing sufficient new experiences to motivate return visits.
  • The significant increase in "Expense" (+8pp) indicates a growing perception of Abu Dhabi as an expensive destination, which may be related to economic conditions in Kazakhstan.
  • The increase in "Perception as Destination for Wealthy" (+6pp) reinforces the expense concern and suggests a growing image problem among middle-income Kazakhstani travelers.
  • These worsening barriers should be prioritized in marketing communications, with particular focus on addressing value perception and highlighting new experiences that would appeal to repeat visitors.
  • The combined effect of the expense-related barriers suggests a need for a comprehensive strategy to address value perception in this market.

Improving Barriers

Key positive changes

Barriers to visitation that have shown improvement over time, representing areas of progress.

Improving Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q1 2021):
  • Visa Process: 22% (-5pp) find the visa process difficult
  • Flight Availability: 18% (-3pp) limited direct flights or inconvenient connections
  • Restrictive Laws and Customs: 12% (-2pp) concerned about cultural restrictions
Business Implications:
  • The decrease in "Visa Process" concerns (-5pp) represents a positive shift in perception, likely reflecting the impact of recent visa process improvements for Kazakhstani travelers.
  • The slight decrease in "Flight Availability" concerns (-3pp) suggests some improvement in air connectivity between Kazakhstan and Abu Dhabi.
  • The minor decrease in "Restrictive Laws and Customs" (-2pp) indicates a small positive shift in perception regarding cultural restrictions.
  • Marketing should leverage the positive change in perception regarding visa processes, highlighting the simplified procedures now available to Kazakhstani travelers.
  • These improving barriers demonstrate that perceptions can be changed through targeted communications and policy changes, providing a model for addressing the currently worsening barriers.
  • The improvements in practical barriers (visa, flights) suggest that the focus should now shift to addressing perception barriers (expense, lack of new experiences).

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Kazakhstan. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Kazakhstan market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Low but growing awareness (30.01%, +5.01pp) with low consideration (13.72%) and intent (8.66%), indicating significant opportunity for growth
  • A good intent-to-consideration ratio (0.63) indicating reasonable funnel conversion once consideration is achieved
  • Strong performance metrics with 72,131 hotel guests (+40.6% YoY), 12,441 flight searches (+10.1% YoY), and travel queries growth of 29.0% YoY
  • A relatively short average stay (3.3 days) indicating short-break vacation patterns
  • A moderate booking window (49 days) allowing for effective campaign planning
  • Bi-modal seasonality with peaks in March and October
  • Low media elasticity (0.09) suggesting limited response to media investments alone
  • Strong interest in beach experiences and theme parks, with Saadiyat Beach and SeaWorld Abu Dhabi being the most searched attractions
  • Strong preference for Rixos properties, with Rixos Marina Abu Dhabi generating more than twice the page views of any other accommodation
  • Significant barriers including expense (41%), previous visitation (24%), and visa process (22%)
  • Concerning growth in expense perception (+8pp) and previous visitation (+12pp) as barriers

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Continuing strong awareness-building efforts as the primary focus for this emerging market
  • Addressing the perception of Abu Dhabi as an expensive destination through value-focused messaging and affordable package options
  • Highlighting new attractions and experiences to encourage repeat visitation
  • Leveraging the strong preference for Rixos properties through co-marketing partnerships
  • Emphasizing the combination of beach experiences and entertainment options that clearly appeal to this market
  • Developing compact itineraries that maximize the typical 3.3-day stay, focusing on high-impact experiences
  • Timing campaigns to align with the 49-day booking window, particularly for the March and October peak periods
  • Creating marketing content that showcases the family-friendly entertainment options that are of high interest to this market
  • Continuing to communicate improvements in the visa process and flight connectivity
  • Positioning Abu Dhabi as offering good value for money to counter the growing perception of expense
  • Emphasizing the diversity of experiences available to maximize engagement during the short 3.3-day average stay duration

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Kazakhstan market. The significant growth in awareness (+5.01pp) provides a solid foundation for building the marketing funnel, though continued investment is required to reach moderate awareness levels.
